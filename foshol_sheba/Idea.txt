Project Name
Foshol-Sheba: Intelligent Crop Health & Diagnostics System

Description
Foshol-Sheba is a smart, mobile-responsive web platform designed to empower farmers with immediate, AI-driven crop health diagnostics. By capturing an image of a crop leaf, the system utilizes advanced convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to identify diseases in real-time, providing farmers with actionable treatment suggestions and preventative measures. The platform bridges the gap between traditional farming and modern precision agriculture by offering expert-validated solutions through a simple, accessible interface.

Stakeholders
Farmers: The primary users who interact with the app to diagnose sick crops and receive treatment guidance.

Agricultural Experts/Agronomists: Technical validators who ensure the accuracy of the disease-to-treatment mapping and provide professional input on evolving crop health trends.

Fertilizer & Pesticide Companies: Commercial partners who provide data on approved, region-specific products to support the treatment recommendation engine.

System Administrators: Responsible for maintaining the server, updating the AI model, and managing user data privacy.

Government/Research Bodies: Entities that utilize aggregated, anonymized data to monitor regional disease outbreaks.

Features
Core Functionality:

AI-Powered Detection: CNN-based model for instant identification of common crop diseases with confidence-level display.

Actionable Recommendations: Tailored advice, including organic remedies, chemical treatments, and preventative cultural practices.

Multi-Modal Upload: Direct camera capture and gallery upload for flexibility in the field.

Bilingual Interface: Full support for both Bangla and English to ensure maximum accessibility for local farmers.

User-Centric Tools:

Diagnostic History: A personalized log for farmers to monitor the health status of their crops over time.

Responsive Design: Optimized for low-bandwidth mobile environments, ensuring the app works effectively in rural areas.

Admin Dashboard: A centralized portal for managing the disease database and monitoring system activity.

Future Work
Offline Capability: Integrating Service Workers (PWA) to allow image capturing and queuing for analysis when connectivity is restored.

Advanced Analytics: Integration of IoT sensors for real-time soil moisture and temperature monitoring to provide context-aware disease warnings.

Community Forum: A peer-to-peer section for farmers to share experiences and localized solutions.

Market Price Integration: Real-time data feed from the Department of Agricultural Marketing to help farmers make informed decisions about crop sales.

Expanded Crop Coverage: Scaling the dataset to include a broader range of major crops and regional varieties.

Blockchain Integration: Implementing a secure, decentralized ledger to protect sensitive agricultural data and maintain an immutable record of disease outbreaks.
